Post 01 Storytelling
11pm. My phone rang.
It was a client. Not my client — someone else's. He had signed two weeks earlier. Paid 180,000 AED upfront. 2% commission. No questions asked. The developer had gone completely silent. No updates. No calls back. Nothing. He wasn't naive. He wasn't rushing. He just trusted the wrong person. That's the part that stays with me. We spent three hours on the phone going through everything — contracts, options, next steps. We found a way forward. But I kept thinking: this conversation should have happened before he signed. Not after. Here's what I've learned: the unit is almost never the risk. The developer is. Do your homework. Or work with someone who already has.
Post 02 Authority — 3 Mistakes Buyers Make
Three mistakes I see buyers make in Dubai — every single week.
Not because they're careless. Because nobody warned them. 1. They fall in love with the view, not the numbers. The apartment looks stunning. Then the yield comes in at 4% on an overpriced asset. Always run the numbers before you run to the showroom. 2. They wait for the "right time." I've watched people sit on the sidelines for 18 months waiting for prices to dip. They're now paying 30% more. Dubai doesn't wait for hesitation. 3. They trust the project. Not the developer. A beautiful brochure is easy to produce. A clean delivery record is not. Ask how many projects they've completed. Ask for actual handover data. Buying in Dubai can be one of the best decisions you make — or one of the most expensive. The difference is almost always in the preparation.

Post 03 Unpopular Opinion
I've talked clients out of buying more times than I've sold to them.
Wrong developer. Wrong entry price. Wrong area for their actual goal. Those conversations cost me commission. I had them anyway. Here's something most people in this industry won't say out loud: most buyers don't need a real estate agent. They need an advisor. An agent's job is to close. An advisor's job is to protect you from the wrong close. Those are completely different things. I've never pushed a deal I didn't believe in. Not because I'm noble — because I've seen what happens when someone buys on borrowed confidence. They lose money. They lose trust. They never forgive themselves. The clients who listened to my "walk away" advice always came back. With bigger budgets. With referrals. With loyalty no commission can manufacture. Honesty is the longest game in this market. It's also the best one.

Post 05 Educational — Off-Plan vs Ready
Every week someone asks me: off-plan or ready property?
Every week I give the same answer. Wrong question. Off-plan is not better. Ready is not safer. They're different tools — and the wrong tool for your situation is always a bad deal. Off-plan makes sense when: You have patience, a lower entry price in mind, and a 2–3 year horizon. And you've verified the developer actually delivers on time. Ready makes sense when: You want rental income now. You want to see exactly what you're buying. You can't afford delays — financially or emotionally. The simple breakdown: — End-user moving to Dubai → Ready. Always. — Investor with 3-year outlook + verified developer → Off-plan. — Investor who needs yield today → Ready. No question. Stop asking which is better. Start asking which is better for you.
Post 06 Trust-Building — First-Time Buyers
If you're buying in Dubai for the first time — slow down.
Not because the market is dangerous. Because the excitement of this city makes people move faster than they should. You will see stunning units. You will meet charming agents. You will feel urgency that may or may not be real. Ask better questions. Not just "what's the price per sqft" — but: Who is the developer? What's their delivery record? What's the service charge history? What does exit look like in three years? A property in Dubai is not just a home or an investment. It's a relationship with a developer, a community, a management company, and a market that moves fast and doesn't give refunds. Work with someone who'll tell you the truth. Not someone who tells you what you want to hear to close the deal. First-time buyers don't need more options. They need better guidance.
